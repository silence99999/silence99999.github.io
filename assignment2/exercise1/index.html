Tribute to Nursultan Nazarbayev

About Nursultan Nazarbayev

Nursultan Nazarbayev (born 1940) is a Kazakh politician who served as the first President of Kazakhstan from 1991 to 2019. He played a key role in guiding Kazakhstan after the dissolution of the Soviet Union and in shaping its political and economic development.

Key Achievements

  1. 1991 - Became the first President of independent Kazakhstan.
  2. 1997 - Moved the capital from Almaty to Astana (now Nur-Sultan).
  3. 1999 - Introduced major economic reforms to stabilize the country.
  4. 2010 - Became the "Leader of the Nation" (Elbasy).
  5. 2019 - Announced his resignation after nearly 30 years in power.

Contributions

  • Promoted Kazakhstans nuclear disarmament.
  • Strengthened international relations and diplomacy.
  • Modernized Kazakhstans economy and industry.
  • Founded multiple international peace and cooperation initiatives.
